Beginer Tips

1) Youtube Is Your Friend

The best way to learn how to play D&D is by watching tutorials on Youtube. You have a massive of content to watch, ranging from campaigns or different methods of gameplay. Whats even better about using this platform, it's free! You may also discover, just like me, many celebrities like Vin Diesel and Terry Crews are fellow role players.

2)Search for pre-made characters if you dont have the players hand book

Making a character at first may seem like a fun idea, but with very little knowledge, can be very daunting. Luckily however, with the power of the internet, people and even wizards of the coast, regulary upload documents ready to download at your convenience.
If you are thinking about purchasing the starter set, youll be pleased to know that the set contains 6 pre-generated character sheets and a couple of blank ones to update for the future.

Use A Pre-made Adventure

One of the joys of the community is that they love publishing their own adventures with different difficulties for beginners and veterans. For a beginner DM, these adventures allow players to have quick access, for free, to an adventure readily. A great intro campaign to teach both the new DM and players is, The Death Pit of Moloch. The story is very straight forward for everyone and demonstrates all the mechanics of the game, with information at the ready.
